Research

  • Mechanisms of intron loss and gain in fission yeasts.

    The fission yeast, Schizosaccharomyces, is on the basal branch of the Ascomycota phylum. It has much more introns than the budding yeast, but fewer than Aspergillus. Some previous studies show that reverse transcriptase mediated intron loss events dominate in fission yeasts, however evidence from recent intron changes is missing. Now, we will take you into the world of fission yeasts in which intron losses, intron gains, intronization and exonization happens in the recent 200 million years. This is a normal interspecific genomic analysis with no much amazing discoveries. The only goal is to convince us that intron loss really happens in fission yeast, even in recent years.

  • Correlation of intron loss rate and processed pseudogene abundance in rodents.

    Reverse transcription plays an important role in precise intron deletion. Some evidence includes adjacent intron loss, 3'-side intron loss, germline expression and/or high expression level of intron-lost genes. Here we propose a new evidence that intron-lost genes tend to generate more processed pseudogenes, which are the byproducts of reverse transcription. Just one more evidence, not the best evidence!
