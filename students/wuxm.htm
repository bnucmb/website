    RESEARCH EXPERIENCES

    Generally, I focus on applying the tools of bioinformatics, graph theory and statistics to the studies of biological networks and protein functions.

    • Developed the project of 'genome-wide reconstruction of yeast protein-protein interaction network based on Gene Ontology' (Feb. 2005-Apr. 2006)

      • Solely derived from the Gene Ontology (GO), we have defined an effective method, named Relative Specificity Similarity (RSS) for measuring the semantic similairty between two GO terms. Using the RSS method, we reconstructed a yeast protein interaction network based on the GO and yeast annotations. Moreover, we obtained a gold standard positive (GSP) dataset and a gold standard negative (GSN) dataset of protein interactions. The two datasets, especially the GSN, are important for a statistical analysis system.

    Then, based on the release of GO (March 2006 ) and yeast annotations derived from SGD (March 31, 2006), we developed a web-accessible database SPIDer (Saccharomyces protein-protein interaction database): http://cmb.bnu.edu.cn/SPIDer/index.html. SPIDer provides a variety of search options and graphical visualization of a network. Interestingly, users can retrieve the inter-member interactions in a list of proteins or in a protein complex of interest.

    • Developing the project of 'characterizing hub proteins by projecting yeast protein-protein interaction network onto four cell cycle phases' (Nov. 2007-present, ONGOING)

    Currently, we focus on dynamic decomposition and analysis of yeast protein-protein interaction network. Through temporally decomposing an integrated yeast protein interaction network into four cell cycle phases, we want to answer one question: does a hub in the aggregated cell cycle interaction network still act as hubs in all or one of the four phase sub-networks? Our main conclusion is that not all the hubs in the aggregated cell cycle network still act as hubs in all the decomposed phase sub-networks. The hubs observed in the aggregated network are just an assemblage of all the binary interactions of the hubs during different time series.

    • Responsible for the project of 'GO-based functional categorization of genes' (Jul. 2006-Jan. 2007)

    To obtain the best GO terms categorizing a list of genes of interest, several statistical methods are widely used, such as fisher's exact test and binomial test. We tried to design an automatic approach using GO to help with the biological interpretation of the list of genes. Compared with the other tools using the classical methods, our approach unluckily shows no advantage.

    • Participated in the project of 'genome-wide inference of interacting motif pairs' (Oct. 2006-Dec. 2007)

    An approach was designed to predicting high-confidence interacting motif pairs that are significantly over-represented in a high-confidence protein interaction (GSP) dataset compared to non-interaction (GSN) dataset. The quality of four negative datasets was evaluated and compared. Of these, the one with the best performance was considered as the GSN dataset. In the project, one part of my work is to construct three negative datasets, two datasets derived from the RSS method and the other one comprising protein pairs in different subcellular compartments.

    • Participating in the project of 'classification of protein sequences by decomposing protein domain architecture network' (May 2007-present, ONGOING)

    Many current methods of classification of protein sequences are depend on sequence alignments, but establish incorrect families due to multiple domain features of proteins. To address this problem, we constructed a protein domain architecture network based on our previous work (Lin et al, Bioinformatics 2006), and designed a new method for protein classification by decomposing the network. The method was applied on 511,203 eukaryotic proteins which were dispatched into 6,797 protein groups. The groups were evaluated using the Eukaryotic Orthologous Groups of Proteins (KOGs) and GO annotations, indicating that proteins in each group evolutionarily manifest their homologous relationships. In the project, my work is mainly to evaluate the functional similarity within a protein group based on the GO annotations.
